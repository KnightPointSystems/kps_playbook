                            <h6>Practices</h6>
                            <h1 class="bar-top">Group Interview</h1>
                            <p class="usa-font-lead">
                                Meeting with a group of individuals offers you an opportunity to collect a diverse set of viewpoints.
                            </p>
                        </div>
                    </div>
                </div>
            </section>

            <!-- description -->
            <div class="usa-section">
                <p>
                    In a group interview, a number of individuals are brought together to gather their feedback. Group interviews help create a space where a diverse set of viewpoints can be shared. They can be a great way to expand the scope of the conversation compared to a single user interview.
                </p>
            </div>
            <!-- //description -->


            <!-- procedure -->
            <div class="usa-section">
                <h3>Procedure</h3>
                <fieldset class="usa-fieldset-inputs usa-sans">
                    <ul class="usa-unstyled-list">
                        <li>
                            <input type="checkbox" unchecked>
                            <label>
                                Decide the group you want to interview. Determine the makeup of the group, taking into account viewpoint need or chemistry of participants.
                            </label>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                            <input type="checkbox" unchecked>
                            <label>
                                Find a neutral space where individuals who may not feel comfortable speaking on their own can be engaged. If possible a neutral ground for the users, such as a conference room or cafeteria space.
                            </label>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                            <input type="checkbox" unchecked>
                            <label>
                                Have only one person asking questions and the rest of the team members who are participating, taking notes and capturing what the group is saying.
                            </label>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                            <input type="checkbox" unchecked>
                            <label>
                                Be strategic in placing people together to unlock an expanded collective view of user needs. Engage quieter participants by asking them direct questions.
                            </label>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                            <input type="checkbox" unchecked>
                            <label>
                                At the end of the session, download what was learned from the group. Group findings by theme or commonality.
                            </label>
                        </li>
                    </ul>
                </fieldset>
            </div>
